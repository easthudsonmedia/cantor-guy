600 Chariots

Hebrew Liturgy Turned Symphony

The symphonic evocations of 600 Chariots brings the stories of the Bible to life through a unique combination of classical music and pop. This liturgical music is sung in Hebrew and translated to English so all can experience the spiritual enveloping that takes place when you listen to these complex musical arrangements. Many musicians throughout the years have employed great orchestras to give full voice to their musical vision. Lacking those resources, I turned to technology to fill the seats of my orchestra.

Lyrics

Free, by the sea they gathered
And lifted up their weary eyes.
Six hundred chariots
Were charging in the distance.
An angel of God stood up to guard them,
With a pillar of cloud all through the night.

Who is like You in godliness, Adonai?
Who is like You, mighty in holiness?
Awesomely glorified, doing wonders.

Free, like the strong east wind
That pushed the sea all through the night,
Six hundred chariots
Were racing down on dry land.
The water that stood collapsed with sunrise,
And droplets flowed like their tears of freedom.

Your children saw Your majesty:
Splitting the sea before Moses and Miriam.
"This is my God," they replied,
And said, "Adonai will reign forever."

Lay us down peacefully, Adonai our God.
And raise us up restoring life, our Guardian.
And spread over us Your peaceful shelter.
And well-counseled, prepare us to face You
And save us, for Your name's sake.

And protect us by removing from our midst:
Foe, plague, and sword, and hunger, and grief.
And keep us away from misdeed and transgression,
So we might be sheltered beneath the shadow of Your wing.

Because You are our protecting, and rescuing God.
Because You are a pardoning and compassionate God.
And guard our going and our coming
To life and peace from now to eternity.

Blessed are You Adonai,
Who spreads a peaceful shelter
Upon us, Your entire People of Israel,
And upon Jerusalem.

The People of Israel shall keep the Sabbath,
To make it an eternal covenant through all generations.
Between Me and the People of Israel, it is an eternal sign,
Because in six days God created the heavens and the earth.
And on the seventh day, ceased and rested.

The People who sanctify the seventh day,
Shall all be satisfied and find pleasure in Your goodness.
And You wished for the seventh day to be sanctified.
You called it the most desired of days.
A remnant of the very first deed.
Those who keep the Sabbath and delight in it,
Shall rejoice in Your realm.

God, how can I find You? You are supreme and mysterious.
But how can I not find You? You fill the entire world.
I seek Your closeness. I cry out for You wholeheartedly.
But as I yearn for You, I ultimately find You yearning for me.
Blessed are You, Adonai, the holy God.

We thank You, for You are Adonai our God
And the God of our fathers and mothers forever.
You are our life's protector,
The safeguard of our salvation,
From generation to generation.
We will thank You and apprise Your glory,
For our lives, surrendered in Your hands.

For our souls, which are at Your command,
And for the miracles, which we encounter daily.
For Your wonders and goodness,
At each moment, evening, morning and noon.

The Benevolent, Your mercies never cease.
The Merciful, Your graces never cease.
Blessed are You, Adonai,
"The Benevolent" is Your name,
And it is pleasing to thank You.

Bestow abundant peace upon Israel, Your people, forever.
For You are a sovereign, predominating peace.
And You are pleased to bless Your People Israel,
At each moment and each hour, with Your peace.
Blessed are You, Adonai,
Who blesses the People Israel with peace.

My God, guard my tongue against evil
And my lips from speaking deceitfully.
May my soul be unshaken by my slanders
And be humbled to all, as the dust of the earth.

Open my heart to receive Your teaching,
And may my soul pursue Your commandments.
And all who think poorly of me,
Hastily, rescind their opinion and hinder their thought.

Do for the sake of Your name.
Do for the sake of Your right hand.
Do for the sake of Your holiness.
Do for the sake of Your Torah.

So that Your devotees be rescued,
Grant salvation by Your right hand,
And answer me.

Soul's beloved, Merciful parent,
Draw Your servant to Your will.
Your servant will run like a dear,
Will bow before Your splendor.
For Your love is tastier
Than sweet nectar and all other flavors.
Splendid, Fair, World's brilliance, My soul is lovesick.

Please, God, cure her
By showing her your pleasant brilliance.
Then she will gain strength and be cured
And will be Your servant forever.
Enduring one, may your mercy kindle,
To pardon your loving child. For Your child is yearning, longing,
To witness Your majestic might.

Please, God, my heart's delight,
Haste, do not hide.
Please reveal Yourself and spread over me The shelter of Your peace.
The earth will light up with Your glory.
We shall all rejoice and exalt You.
Haste, beloved, the time has come
And have mercy on me like days of old.

Go forth, my beloved, towards the bride. Let us greet the Sabbath.

"Keep" and "Remember" in one commandment,
Uttered by the absolute God.
Adonai is one, unified in name, splendor, and glory.

Towards Shabbat, go forth, and we shall follow,
For it is the source of blessing.
Reigning from the beginning of time, initiated with much intention.

Arrive peacefully, crowned by your initiator,
In joy and merriment.
Through treasured people's faith. Arrive, bride! Arrive, bride!
